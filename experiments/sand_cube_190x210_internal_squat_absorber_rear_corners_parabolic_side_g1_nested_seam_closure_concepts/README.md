# Nested-seam closure concepts

This isolated experiment preserves the exact unsplit parabolic enclosure as
the authoritative assembled exterior.  The baffle plug steps inward behind
the original tangent seam, while the bucket retains a continuous outer lip
that conceals the mating taper.  A 0.28 mm hidden fit allowance separates the
socket surfaces, and the existing continuous 5 mm wide, 2 mm thick foam gasket
remains farther inside.

It compares four lower retention concepts while retaining the two hidden top
hooks:

- two concealed 4.8 mm zip-tie loops through sealed dry-side saddles, with the
  tie heads recessed flush into the underside;
- two sacrificial zip latches: each baffle captures an intact tie head, its
  tail runs through a roofed floor tunnel, and a replaceable second ratchet
  head sits in a flush underside clipping pocket;
- two open underside M4 bolt/nut channels, with hardware captured behind
  opposing bucket and baffle collars;
- two reinforced 45 degree dry-side pocket screws with a modeled sealant or
  O-ring annulus at each underside entry.

The loop, bolt, and pocket-screw passages remain ahead of the gasket plane.
The longer sacrificial-latch tail tunnel stays below the gasket loop and has a
modeled 2 mm sealed roof separating it from the enclosure air.  Neither path
crosses the compressed foam seal.

These are mechanism concepts, not final hardware contracts.  Purchased zip
ties, bolts, nuts, inserts, and tool-access dimensions still require fit
coupons before printing a complete enclosure.
