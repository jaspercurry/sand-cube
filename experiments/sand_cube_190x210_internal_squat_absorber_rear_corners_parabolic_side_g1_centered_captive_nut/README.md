# Centered captive-nut closure experiment

This isolated child of the committed nested-seam closure concepts keeps the
authoritative unsplit parabolic exterior, continuous 5 x 2 mm gasket, hidden
upper hooks, full internal assembly, and validated port system.

The lower closure is one centered M4 screw rising rearward at 10 degrees from
the bottom normal.  Its 8.5 mm head is fully recessed by 0.25 mm in a true
9.2 mm angled flat-bottom counterbore, rather than a vertical pocket with an
incorrect bearing surface.

A hidden 13.0 mm bucket tongue locally doglegs the split so the screw head
bears entirely on the bucket.  A standard M4 hex nut drops edge-on through a
hidden top slot, rotates into its anti-rotation baffle seat, and bears entirely
on a 12.0 mm baffle-owned load pad.  Tightening therefore draws two different
printed parts together.  Both audited bearing ratios are 100 percent; the
minimum bucket head wall is 1.747 mm and the baffle pad is 1.5 mm thick.

The screw passage and nut trap remain outside the gasket loop with 2.25 mm
minimum dry-side clearance, so the underside opening is not an acoustic leak
path.  The bucket tongue reassigns existing shell material and adds no
exterior blister.  The baffle load pad adds only 14.882 mm3, is hidden on the
dry side, and is rooted into 130.908 mm3 of existing baffle material.

The generated output includes assembled, exploded, true cutaway, local joint
section, bucket-only, and baffle-only viewers plus STEP round-trip diagnostics.
